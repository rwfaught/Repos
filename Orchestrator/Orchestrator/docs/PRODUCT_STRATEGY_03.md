# PRODUCT_STRATEGY_03.md

## Product Strategy 03: First Proving-Ground Subdomain Selection

---

## Goal

Select the first concrete proving-ground subdomain inside the broader product-entry wedge established in `PRODUCT_STRATEGY_02.md`.

This artifact exists to answer a narrower question than before:

**Inside consequential document-and-admin casework, which first subdomain most clearly proves the value of local bounded-trust orchestration over both raw AI and ordinary workflow tools?**

This is a **strategy artifact**.

It is not:
- a feature phase
- a product roadmap
- an implementation packet
- a market-sizing memo
- a brand-copy exercise

Its purpose is narrower:

to choose the first proving-ground subdomain that should shape future ranking, demos, and design decisions.

---

## Why This Artifact Exists

The project now has a clearer product structure:

- Ro as engine
- consequential document-and-admin casework as first proving-ground family
- adjacent expansion families identified but not primary

That was necessary.

But the proving-ground family is still too broad to guide concrete ranking cleanly.

“Document-and-admin casework” still includes too many different surfaces:
- insurance claims
- billing disputes
- reimbursement packets
- benefits appeals
- contractor/home-issue casework
- compliance/document disputes
- small-business admin disputes

If the project does not narrow further, future ranking can still drift because too many proposals can claim relevance.

This artifact exists to reduce that ambiguity.

---

## Core Strategic Question

The governing question is now:

**Which first subdomain makes the engine’s advantages most obvious to a normal person, while remaining bounded enough to guide real product choices?**

The subdomain should be narrow enough to matter and broad enough to prove the engine rather than a one-off gimmick.

---

## Required Strategic Outcome

This artifact must force explicit answers to these questions:

1. Which subdomain should be the first proving ground?
2. Why does it demonstrate local state, bounded trust, resumability, inspectability, and tracked autonomy more clearly than the alternatives?
3. What alternatives should remain secondary for now?
4. What future work becomes easier or harder to justify once that subdomain is chosen?

If the artifact does not narrow to one leading subdomain or a very tight ranked pair, it is too soft.

---

## This Artifact Is NOT About

This artifact is NOT about:

- choosing a final forever market
- building the implementation immediately
- writing landing-page copy
- proving that other subdomains are invalid forever
- resuming broad feature work
- abstractly celebrating “many possibilities”

This artifact is about choosing the first proving ground sharply enough to constrain ranking.

---

## Subdomain Selection Criteria

The first subdomain should score highly on all or most of these:

- understandable to a normal person immediately
- consequential enough that partial correctness is dangerous
- document-heavy enough that evidence tracking matters
- long enough in duration that resumability matters
- messy enough that local state feels like relief
- personal enough that bounded model exposure feels meaningful
- structured enough that early implementation can stay bounded
- repeated-use plausible enough that success would validate the repeated-use test

This selection must optimize for legibility, not just technical compatibility.

---

## Current Leading Candidate

The current leading candidate is:

**claims / disputes / appeals casework for consequential personal or small-business paperwork problems**

This includes cases such as:
- insurance claims
- billing disputes
- reimbursement disputes
- benefits appeals
- similar documentation-driven casework where the user is managing evidence, drafts, and follow-up over time

This is the strongest candidate because it makes the engine’s core promises legible all at once:
- local state matters
- bounded trust matters
- resumability matters
- tracked actions matter
- inspectability matters
- selective model routing matters
- human approval matters without requiring constant micromanagement

---

## Why This Candidate Leads

### 1. It makes the burden obvious
Normal people immediately understand the pain of carrying a messy dispute or claim across time.
This is not theoretical.

### 2. It makes “mostly right” visibly dangerous
A half-right answer can create more downstream mess:
- wrong timeline
- missing evidence
- bad draft
- missed deadline
- contradictory statement

That makes the harness easier to justify.

### 3. It makes privacy and bounded exposure feel real
People intuitively understand why they may not want one remote system to see the entire case at once.

### 4. It supports bounded early implementation
The system can begin with:
- document ingestion
- fact extraction
- timeline assembly
- missing-evidence tracking
- bounded drafting
- clarification/approval points

without pretending to solve every bureaucratic workflow on day one.

### 5. It has repeated-use plausibility
If it helps once, a person or small business is likely to want it again the next time a similar mess appears.

---

## Secondary Candidates

These remain strategically credible, but secondary for now:

### 1. Moving / life-transition management
Strong for average-person legibility, but broader and less sharply consequential in some cases.

### 2. Small-business vendor / invoice / back-office disputes
Very strong, but may be slightly less universal as a first emotional proving ground.

### 3. Resumable research pipelines
Architecturally coherent, but less immediately legible to a normal person who does not already value structured knowledge work.

### 4. Bounded coding / repo workflows
Still important as an engine proof domain, but too strategically crowded and too likely to collapse the product back into the coding-proof cage.

These should remain secondary until the first proving ground is clearer.

---

## Recommended Narrowing Rule

This artifact should adopt the following rule:

**The first proving ground is claims/disputes/appeals-style casework, not “all paperwork” and not “all admin.”**

That is the right level of sharpness for now.

It is narrow enough to guide ranking.
It is broad enough to prove the engine pattern rather than one quirky niche.

---

## Future Ranking Consequence

If this artifact is ratified, future work should now be judged against this question:

**Does this make Ro better at handling claims/disputes/appeals-style casework as the first proving ground, while still strengthening the underlying engine?**

That means:

### Easier to justify
- local case-state handling
- evidence tracking
- document ingestion and extraction
- missing-material detection
- timeline assembly
- bounded document drafting
- selective routing between cheaper and stronger models
- approval/clarification thresholds
- resumable case progress

### Harder to justify
- broad assistant behavior
- workflow generalization before proving-ground success
- flashy interface expansion
- more internal machinery not tied to casework relief
- codebase-only optimizations that do not also strengthen the proving ground
- product moves that widen the story before this wedge is proven

This is the practical narrowing effect the artifact must produce.

---

## What This Artifact Must Produce

This strategy artifact must produce explicit answers to:

1. the first proving-ground subdomain
2. why it beats the alternatives right now
3. which alternatives remain secondary
4. what future work is now easier to justify
5. what future work should be deprioritized until the proving ground is proven

If it cannot do that, it is still too abstract.

---

## Deliverable Shape

This artifact should end in:

1. a named first proving-ground subdomain
2. a brief justification for why it leads
3. a ranked secondary shortlist
4. a clear ranking consequence
5. a narrowed test for future proposals

It does not yet need to authorize implementation.

---

## Validation Standard

This artifact is acceptable only if it:

- narrows the proving ground enough to affect ranking
- preserves Ro as an engine rather than collapsing it into a one-off tool
- keeps the chosen subdomain legible to a normal person
- makes the harness’s value easier to explain in lived experience rather than architecture jargon
- makes some future work clearly lower-priority

---

## Expected Next Step After This Artifact

If this artifact is ratified, the likely next move would be one of:

- a design artifact for autonomy / interruption thresholds shaped by the proving ground
- a design artifact for case-state structure aligned to the proving ground
- a strategic re-ranking pass using the chosen subdomain as the active product filter

But normal implementation work should still remain paused until the first subdomain choice is accepted as the live ranking membrane.
