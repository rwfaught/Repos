# OWNER_AUTHORED_SYSTEM_PRINCIPLE.md

## Principle

Orchestrator may begin as the operator's personally sovereign system while preserving seams so other operators can eventually make it their own.

This principle is:
- personal at point of use
- adoptable at point of transfer

## Constraints

- Personal does not mean hardcoded.
- Portable does not mean generic.
- Customizable does not mean loose.
- Local-first does not mean isolated from other people's use cases.

Orchestrator should not merely be Roger's workflow.
Orchestrator should become a framework for owner-authored workflows.
The intended stance is: "my system" at point of use and "their system" at point of adoption.

## Future Seams (Not Authorized Build Scope)

Potential seams include:
- local policy
- packet templates
- approval thresholds
- model preferences
- transport adapters
- workflow definitions
- operator-specific defaults
- privacy posture
- escalation rules
- case/work packet types

## Explicit Non-Authorization

This principle document does not authorize:
- a customization framework
- a plugin marketplace
- multi-user support
- a public template ecosystem
- a UI theme/config system
- a broad abstraction phase
- a generic workflow engine
